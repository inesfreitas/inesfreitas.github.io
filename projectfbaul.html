rethinking fbaul’s website.

the challenge.

The main challenge was the creation of a new proposal for the FBAUL website, focused on a user segment, capable of improving the user experience.

The process included 4 stages: analysis and discovery; ideation and development of solutions; usability tests; synthesis and presentation of results.

our segment.

Our segment was international students, which was divided into 3 sub-segments: Erasmus and short-term students; international Portuguese-speaking students; international Portuguese-non-speaking students.

Our proposal focuses on international Portuguese-non-speaking students, the sub-segment for which the current website presents more gaps and problems.

analysis and discovery.

primary research

secondary research

We collected data from reports about international students in Portugal’s universities and the usage of FBAUL’s website, benchmark on other universities (national and international).

We then mapped the site and used a survey to get first hand insights on how the international students interacted with the site.

user mapping.

We created 3 diferent proto-personas, each for one of our identified sub-sugments, in order to map how they interact with the website and which pain points are common to all of them through empathy maps and a customer journey map of the application process for the international Portuguese-non-speaking students.

ideation and development of solutions.

We then settled on 4 How Might We’s and started the decision making process relative to what kind of information was most important to international students and based on that we restructured the entire information architecture of the site.

how might we:

Review the architecture and navigation to become more synthetic, organized and intuitive.

Create an international students section, with more dedicated and comprehensive information.

Create a more appealing homepage, with updated, relevant and strategic information.

Restructure existing information for international students, in a more intuitive and organized way, considering the overall architecture.
